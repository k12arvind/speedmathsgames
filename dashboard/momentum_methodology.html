1. Mark Minervini — Primary Methodology

Mark Minervini

US Trading Champion (1997), Author of "Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard" and "Think & Trade Like a Champion"
  • IMPLEMENTED Trend Template (8 criteria): Price > SMA150 & SMA200, SMA150 > SMA200, SMA200 rising, SMA50 > SMA150 & SMA200, Price > SMA50, ≥30% above 52W low, within 25% of 52W high, RS ≥ 70. All 8 criteria implemented as vectorized checks in momentum_scanner.py:141-209
  • IMPLEMENTED SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis): Stage 2 uptrend focus — only buy stocks in the accumulation/markup phase. The Trend Template filter ensures only Stage 2 stocks pass.
  • IMPLEMENTED VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern): Progressively tighter price contractions (e.g., 25% → 15% → 8%) with declining volume. Detected via segment analysis in momentum_scanner.py:211-301. First contraction ≤ 35%, final ≤ 15%, volume must decline.
  • IMPLEMENTED Breakout Detection: Price above pivot on volume ≥ 40% above 50-day average. Stop-loss placed 8% below pivot. Implemented in momentum_scanner.py:303-328
  • IMPLEMENTED Relative Strength Rating: Percentile rank of weighted performance composite (40% 3M + 20% 6M + 20% 1Y + 20% 1M). Computed in momentum_scanner.py:124-139
  • IMPLEMENTED VCP Notation Format: "12W 25%-15%-8% / 3T" format (duration, contraction depths, number of contractions) from Minervini's base analysis shorthand.
  • PLANNED Power Play Setup: 100%+ gain in 8 weeks followed by 3-6 week consolidation with max 20% correction. Rare but highest-momentum setup. Planned for Phase 2.
  • PLANNED Progressive Exposure: Start at 25-50% invested, scale up only with winning trades, scale back to cash on 5-7% drawdown. Planned for portfolio tracker module.
  • PLANNED Position Sizing: Max 1-2% account risk per trade. Position Size = (Equity × Risk%) / (Entry - Stop). Planned for portfolio module.
  • RESEARCH Fundamental Screening: EPS growth ≥ 20% quarterly, ≥ 25% annual, revenue growth ≥ 15%, ROE ≥ 17%. Not implemented — TradingView screener provides technical data only; fundamentals would require a separate data source.

2. Indian Market Practitioners

Manas Arora (@iManasArora)

Full-time swing trader since 2012 (15+ years market experience). Speaker at TraderLion Annual Conference 2025. Creator of the Purple Dot indicator. Cash-market-only practitioner — no leverage, no F&O.
  • IMPLEMENTED 6-Point Scanner Template: His stock selection criteria closely mirrors Minervini's Trend Template: (1) Close > Rs 30, (2) Within 25% of 52W high, (3) Close > SMA50 > SMA200, (4) SMA200 rising ≥ 3 months, (5) Close > 50% above 52W low, (6) 52W high achieved within 6 months. Criteria 1-5 are already in our Trend Template. Criterion 6 (recency of 52W high) is a useful additional filter.
  • IMPLEMENTED Volatility Contraction as core setup: Arora's "Momentum Burst" setup is fundamentally a VCP variant — uptrending stocks with strong institutional volume that base/correct in orderly fashion with declining volume. Daily range contracts to < 2-3%, often forming inside bars. This validates our VCP detection logic which looks for progressively tighter contractions.
  • PLANNED Purple Dot Indicator (≥5% move + ≥500K volume): His free TradingView indicator "Combined Up Down with Volume" marks stocks making ≥5% daily moves on ≥500K volume. "More dots = high volatility & liquidity." This is used to build watchlists of "fast movers" for subsequent VCP monitoring. Could be added as a pre-filter or momentum flag in the scanner.
  • PLANNED SVRO Entry Setup: Presented at TraderLion 2025. Strong Start (gap-up showing early strength) + Value Area (price near 21 EMA support) + Relative Volume (high volume confirming) + Opening Range Break. This is an intraday entry timing method on top of a daily VCP — applicable when we add alert/notification features.
  • PLANNED 21 EMA as key support: Price during correction must stay above the 21-day EMA. "Stocks contracting within 1-5% above 21 EMA are prime candidates." A low piercing the 21 EMA is acceptable; a close below is a sell signal. Could be added as a VCP quality filter — current VCP candidates near 21 EMA are higher probability.
  • RESEARCH Prior upmove requirement: Minimum 50% prior upmove backed by institutional volumes before any VCP entry is considered. "Without this upmove, no amount of contraction is enough." Our 30% above 52W low criterion partially captures this but his 50% threshold is more aggressive.
  • RESEARCH "Strong Start" entry timing: Enters within first 1-2% of the move on 5-min/15-min confirmation. A gap-up after a shakeout day or breaking above an inside bar is his potent entry signal. This is intraday execution — not applicable to our daily scanner but relevant for a future alerts module.
  • RESEARCH Aggressive position sizing + pyramiding: Takes large initial positions ("mammoth sized") with 1-2% risk, then doubles down on pullbacks showing same volatility contraction traits. "Sells into strength" — partial exits at 3-5% gains, final chunks on cessation of uptrend. Strike rate ~65-75% in uptrends. Trades typically last 3-4 days.
  • RESEARCH Dead simple stop-loss: Round figure 1-2% from entry OR low of daily candle, whichever is smaller. Break of 21 EMA = definite sell. Simpler than Minervini's 7-8% max stop — reflects his tighter entry timing reducing required stop distance.
  • RESEARCH Small/mid-cap focus: Emphasizes small-cap and mid-cap stocks for higher momentum potential. Consistent with Indian market dynamics where small-caps show more explosive moves than large-caps.
Additional insights from X/Twitter threads:
  • RESEARCH "Risk first. Always." Structures positions so overnight gap-downs can't cause more than 2-3% portfolio damage. Refuses to hold full-size positions overnight. References Minervini: willing to allocate 400% to one stock but would never hold it overnight.
  • RESEARCH "Shakeout before breakout": A false breakdown/shakeout that shakes out weak holders is "majorly responsible for clean breakouts." SS (Strong Start) after a shakeout day is a potent entry signal. Anticipating the breakout rather than chasing it reduces risk.
  • RESEARCH No price targets: Calls target-setting "futile." Remains flexible — "My view can change with the next bar." Sells based on price action deterioration, not pre-set targets.
  • RESEARCH NSE only, ignore indices: Trades only NSE stocks (avoids BSE for lower volume). Does not monitor Nifty/Sensex — "Indices don't generate profits, stocks do." Focuses entirely on individual stock setups.
  • RESEARCH Chart Gym series: Weekly chart annotation exercises where traders mark entries, exits, stops on historical charts. Builds pattern recognition through deliberate practice — "Chart reading muscles."
  • RESEARCH Stock Life Cycle framework: Compares stock stages to tree growth (seed → sapling → mature tree). Helps identify when a stock is in its "growth stage" (equivalent to Minervini's Stage 2) — the optimal time to enter.

4. Data Architecture Decisions

Three data source options were evaluated. The winner was tradingview-screener — an unofficial Python library that provides all needed fields in a single API call.

Data Source Comparison

Source Stocks Speed SMA150 Sector Market Cap Cost Verdict
tradingview-screener 5000+ ~5 sec Yes Yes Yes Free CHOSEN
Zerodha Kite Connect All NSE/BSE Fast No (compute) No No Rs 500/mo No fundamentals
mStock API All NSE/BSE Unknown No No No Free Poor docs, no fundamentals
TradingView Premium N/A N/A N/A N/A N/A $60/mo No data API even with Premium
yfinance (original) ~150 ~15 min Yes (compute) Yes Yes Free Too slow for 5000 stocks

Architecture: Hybrid Approach

Phase Data Source Purpose Stocks Time
Phase 1 tradingview-screener Bulk data: OHLCV, SMAs, 52W range, sector, market cap, performance 5000 ~5 sec
Phase 2 Vectorized pandas Trend Template (8 criteria) + RS rating computation 5000 <1 sec
Phase 3 yfinance 6-month OHLCV for VCP detection on top 100 qualifying stocks ~100 ~30 sec

Total scan time: ~36 seconds (vs ~15 minutes with pure yfinance for just 150 stocks).

Key Design Decisions

  • DATA SMA200 "rising" proxy: TradingView screener provides current SMA values but no historical SMAs. We use SMA150 > SMA200 as a proxy — if the shorter MA is above the longer, the 200 DMA is being pulled upward. (Source: StockManiacs, Chartink community discussions)
  • DATA is_primary filter: TradingView lists stocks on both NSE and BSE. The is_primary == True filter deduplicates to show each stock once (typically the NSE listing).
  • DATA Symbol mapping: TradingView uses underscores (BAJAJ_AUTO), yfinance uses hyphens (BAJAJ-AUTO). ETFs (.RR suffix), InvITs (.PP suffix), and BSE scrip codes (numeric prefixes) are skipped for yfinance VCP checks.
  • DATA F&O stock labeling: F&O stocks have no circuit limits, making them safer for momentum trading. Labeling influenced by StockManiacs and Chartink community advice on avoiding circuit limit traps.
  • DATA VCP limited to top 100: Only the top 100 qualifying stocks (by RS rating) are checked for VCP via yfinance, keeping scan time under 40 seconds. Higher RS stocks are more likely to form quality VCP patterns.
